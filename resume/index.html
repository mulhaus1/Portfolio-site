About Myself

Making the complex easy to understand

The way I describe when asked what is it that I love to do and what I am good at is that I’m passionate about what technology can do, driven to solve problems creatively, disciplined in my approach, respect a development process that works, and I make the complex easy to understand.

It began at the age of 14. I had game envy. The Xbox 360 was the must have device with my friends and all I had was a crap computer I shared with my twin brother. I always had an affinity for figuring out how things work and was determined to get a game on our computer. I did! It didn’t look great, but it worked.

When I look back I see it was one of those aha moments.

It defined my approach to just about everything; there’s no problem that can’t be figured out and solved. Sometimes on my own, often collaborating with others there’s always a way to break it down, find how things fit together, work through possible solutions, and then solve the problem. I learned how to use my curiosity about how things work. Figure it out, use it, get good at it and explain it to others so that they can use it or at the very least understand it. It’s frustrating for someone who is not tech-oriented to listen to our jargon. Friends and clients appreciate my ability to explain things in ways they understand.

As a IT software engineer, I use these talents to challenge myself and to solve problems for clients.
